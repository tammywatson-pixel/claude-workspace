# Workforce Alignment Map

_Last updated: 2026-03-13_

ElevIQ connects individual capability alignment with real education and workforce
opportunities. The goal is to help individuals see how their strengths and motivations
can translate into meaningful learning pathways and careers.

---

## The Three Alignment Layers

ElevIQ operates across three connected layers that bridge the gap between education and employment:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Layer 1: Individual Capability Insights  │
│  Who you are — how you think, learn,      │
│  collaborate, and are motivated           │
└─────────────────┬────────────────────────┘
                  │
┌─────────────────▼────────────────────────┐
│  Layer 2: Learning Pathways               │
│  How you grow — programs, credentials,    │
│  and training that fit your capabilities  │
└─────────────────┬────────────────────────┘
                  │
┌─────────────────▼────────────────────────┐
│  Layer 3: Workforce Opportunities         │
│  Where you thrive — roles, industries,    │
│  and environments aligned to your profile │
└──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

## Layer 1 — Individual Capability Insights

The capability alignment scan surfaces patterns in how an individual:

- Approaches curiosity and exploration
- Solves problems
- Collaborates with others
- Prefers to learn
- Finds motivation

These patterns form a **capability profile** — a starting point for exploring
where the individual is most likely to thrive.

→ See full scan methodology: `memory/capability-alignment-model.md`

---

## Layer 2 — Education Pathways

Capability profiles connect to a range of learning pathways, presenting
**multiple routes** rather than a single rigid path:

| Pathway Type | Examples |
|-------------|---------|
| University programs | Majors aligned to capability patterns and career directions |
| Community college programs | Two-year degrees and transfer pathways |
| Certifications | Industry and technical credentials |
| Workforce training programs | Job-ready skill development |
| Apprenticeships | Learn-and-earn pathways in skilled trades and technical fields |

> The system highlights options rather than prescribing a single direction.

---

## Layer 3 — Workforce Opportunities

Capability insights can help individuals explore career directions including:

| Career Direction | Description |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Technical careers | Engineering, technology, data, skilled trades |
| Professional careers | Business, education, healthcare, public service |
| Entrepreneurship | Venture creation, innovation, self-directed work |
| Skilled trades | Construction, manufacturing, infrastructure |

Focus is on **exploration and alignment** — not rigid classification.

---

## Organizational Use: Workforce Intelligence

Organizations can use aggregated capability insights to better understand their
populations and design stronger programs:

| Use Case | Who Benefits |
|----------|-------------|
| Talent potential mapping | Employers, workforce boards |
| Training needs identification | Educators, program designers |
| Workforce gap analysis | Workforce boards, regional planners |
| Program-to-career alignment | Community colleges, universities |

This is the **Talent Intelligence Layer** — population-level insight built
from individual capability data over time.

→ See: `memory/capability-alignment-model.md` — Talent Intelligence Layer

## Strategic Vision

ElevIQ aims to build a **national framework** that aligns four systems:

```
Individual Capabilities
        +
Education Systems
        +
Workforce Development Programs
        +
Career Pathways
        ↓
Meaningful Work & Purpose-Driven Careers
```

The long-term goal is a replicable, scalable model that any school, workforce
board, or employer can adopt to connect people with opportunities where they
can truly thrive.
